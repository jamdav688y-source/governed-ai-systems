AI Failure Boundary Audit

Fixed-scope diagnostic for AI workflows, agents, and automated decision systems

What this is

The AI Failure Boundary Audit is a focused diagnostic engagement for teams running live or near-live AI systems that can classify, route, approve, notify, publish, trigger actions, or influence downstream execution.

It identifies where a system can continue operating without clear authority, enforceable stop conditions, rollback viability, or replay-grade evidence.

The objective is direct:

determine whether governance is executable, merely advisory, or effectively decorative at the point where decisions become operational.

---

What the audit evaluates

1. Decision surfaces

What the system is allowed to decide, trigger, mutate, or commit.

2. Authority topology

Who owns the decision, who can stop it, who can reverse it, and whether those authorities are actually distinct and enforceable.

3. Stop authority and halt latency

Whether interruption is possible at or below execution speed, or whether control only appears after propagation has already begun.

4. Rollback viability

Whether actions are reversible in practice, within a useful window, by a named owner, with a real recovery path.

5. Evidence and replayability

Whether the system preserves enough evidence to reconstruct what happened: input state, model behavior, decision context, and execution path.

6. Drift and failure boundaries

Where policy drift, authority ambiguity, escalation gaps, or silent continuation risks exist under pressure.

What this is not

This is not:

- a full platform redesign
- ongoing monitoring
- a compliance certification
- a legal opinion
- a broad AI strategy engagement

It is a bounded diagnostic designed to reveal where authority, admissibility, interruption, and reversibility actually hold — and where they break.
